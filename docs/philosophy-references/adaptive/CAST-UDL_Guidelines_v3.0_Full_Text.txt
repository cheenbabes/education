OVERVIEW

The UDL Guidelines aim to foster "learner agency that is purposeful & reflective,
resourceful & authentic, strategic & action-oriented."

The UDL framework is based on extensive research and brain science, offering
design strategies based on three key brain networks:
- Affective Network (impacting motivation and learner engagement)
- Recognition Network (affecting perception and understanding of new learning)
- Strategic Network (addressing learners' abilities to plan, organize, and take action)

Guidelines 3.0 was released July 30, 2024, with emphasis on addressing
"critical barriers rooted in biases and systems of exclusion for learners
with and without disabilities."

Key updates in 3.0:
- A more asset-based approach
- A focus on identity as part of learner variability
- An emphasis on interdependence and collective learning
- A shift from educator-centered to learner-centered language

DETAILED CHECKPOINT DESCRIPTIONS (from Guidelines 2.2 full text, supplemented
with 3.0 updates where noted)

ENGAGEMENT — Welcoming Interests & Identities (Guideline 7)

Optimize choice and autonomy:
Provide learners with as much discretion and autonomy as possible. Offer choices
in areas such as: the level of perceived challenge, the type of rewards or
recognition available, the context or content used for practicing and assessing
skills, the tools used for information gathering or production, the color,
design, or graphics of layouts, and the sequence or timing for completion of
subcomponents of tasks.

Optimize relevance, value, and authenticity:
Vary activities and sources of information so that they can be personalized and
contextualized to learners' lives, culturally relevant and responsive to
different racial, cultural, ethnic, and gender groups, age appropriate and
ability appropriate, and appropriate for different racial, cultural, ethnic,
and linguistic groups.

Nurture joy and play:
Create learning environments that invite curiosity, exploration, and wonder.
Design activities that include elements of surprise, novelty, and delight.
Honor the role of imagination, creativity, and play in learning at all ages.

Address biases, threats, and distractions:
Vary the level of novelty or risk. Vary the level of sensory stimulation.
Vary the social demands required for learning or performance. Involve all
participants in whole class discussions. Create routines. Create an accepting
and supportive classroom climate. Address biases and stereotypes in the
learning environment.

ENGAGEMENT — Sustaining Effort & Persistence (Guideline 8)

Clarify the meaning and purpose of goals:
Prompt or require learners to explicitly formulate or restate goal. Display
the goal in multiple ways. Encourage the setting of personal goals and
objectives. Model the process of setting appropriate goals.

Optimize challenge and support:
Differentiate the degree of difficulty or complexity. Vary the degrees of
freedom for acceptable performance. Emphasize process, effort, improvement
in meeting standards as alternatives to external evaluation and competition.

Foster collaboration, interdependence, and collective learning:
Create cooperative learning groups. Create expectations for group work.
Provide prompts that guide learners in when and how to ask peers and/or
teachers for help. Encourage and support opportunities for peer interactions
and supports. Construct communities of learners.

Foster belonging and community:
Create a safe, inclusive learning environment where all learners feel valued
and respected. Support the development of positive relationships among
learners. Encourage learners to share their perspectives and experiences.

Offer action-oriented feedback:
Provide feedback that encourages perseverance, focuses on development of
efficacy and self-awareness. Provide feedback that is substantive and
informative rather than comparative or competitive. Provide feedback that
models how to incorporate evaluation into positive strategies for future
success.

ENGAGEMENT — Emotional Capacity (Guideline 9)

Recognize expectations, beliefs, and motivations:
Help learners identify and understand their own expectations and beliefs
about learning. Support learners in recognizing how their beliefs affect
their engagement and persistence.

Develop awareness of self and others:
Promote activities that encourage self-reflection and self-assessment.
Develop learners' ability to recognize and cope with their emotions.

Promote individual and collective reflection:
Provide multiple models and scaffolds for managing frustration. Develop
internal controls and coping skills. Use real life situations or simulations
to demonstrate coping skills.

Cultivate empathy and restorative practices:
Support learners in understanding others' perspectives and experiences.
Use restorative approaches to address conflicts and build community.

REPRESENTATION — Perception (Guideline 1)

Support opportunities to customize the display of information:
Offer ways of customizing the display of information including: the size
of text, images, graphs, tables, or other visual content; the contrast
between background and text or image; the color used for information or
emphasis; the volume or rate of speech or sound; the speed or timing of
video, animation, sound, simulations; the layout of visual or other
elements; the font used for print materials.

Support multiple ways to perceive information:
Provide the same information through different sensory modalities
(e.g., through vision, hearing, or touch). Provide information in a
format that will allow for adjustability by the user.

Represent a diversity of perspectives and identities in authentic ways:
Ensure that learning materials include diverse perspectives and identities.
Avoid stereotypical or tokenistic representations.

REPRESENTATION — Language & Symbols (Guideline 2)

Clarify vocabulary, symbols, and language structures:
Pre-teach vocabulary and symbols. Provide descriptions. Provide graphic
symbols with alternative text descriptions. Highlight how complex terms,
expressions, or equations are composed of simpler words or symbols.

Support decoding of text, mathematical notation, and symbols:
Allow the use of text-to-speech. Use automatic voicing with digital
mathematical notation (MathML). Provide digital text with an accompanying
human voice recording. Allow for flexibility and easy access to multiple
representations of notation where appropriate.

Cultivate understanding and respect across languages and dialects:
Make all key information available in dominant languages AND in learners'
home languages. Allow for cross-linguistic glossaries. Embed visual,
non-linguistic supports for vocabulary clarification.

Address biases in the use of language and symbols:
Examine learning materials for linguistic bias. Use inclusive language.
Provide opportunities for learners to contribute terminology from their
own cultures and languages.

Illustrate through multiple media:
Present key concepts in one form of symbolic representation with an
alternative form. Make explicit links between information provided in
texts and any accompanying representation of that information in
illustrations, equations, charts, or diagrams.

REPRESENTATION — Building Knowledge (Guideline 3)

Connect prior knowledge to new learning:
Activate or supply background knowledge. Use advance organizers. Pre-teach
critical prerequisite concepts through demonstration or models. Bridge
concepts with relevant analogies and metaphors. Make explicit cross-
curricular connections.

Highlight and explore patterns, critical features, big ideas, and relationships:
Highlight or emphasize key elements in text, graphics, diagrams, formulas.
Use outlines, graphic organizers, unit organizer routines, concept organizer
routines. Use multiple examples and non-examples. Highlight previously
learned skills that can be used to solve unfamiliar problems.

Cultivate multiple ways of knowing and making meaning:
Provide explicit, supported opportunities to generalize learning. Offer
checklists, organizers, sticky notes, electronic reminders. Offer templates,
graphic organizers, concept maps as scaffolds.

Maximize transfer and generalization:
Provide explicit, supported opportunities to generalize learning to new
situations. Offer checklists, organizers. Offer templates, graphic
organizers, concept maps as scaffolds for linking new information to
prior knowledge.

ACTION & EXPRESSION — Interaction (Guideline 4)

Vary and honor the methods for response, navigation, and movement:
Provide alternatives for physically responding or indicating selections.
Provide alternatives for the rate, timing, speed, and range of motor action
required to interact with instructional materials.

Optimize access to accessible materials and assistive technologies and tools:
Provide alternative keyboard commands for mouse action. Build switch and
scanning access. Provide access to alternative keyboards. Customize overlays
for touch screens and keyboards.

ACTION & EXPRESSION — Expression & Communication (Guideline 5)

Use multiple media for communication:
Compose in multiple media such as text, speech, drawing, illustration,
design, film, music, dance/movement, visual art, sculpture. Use social
media and interactive web tools.

Use multiple tools for construction, composition, and creativity:
Provide spellcheckers, grammar checkers, word prediction software. Provide
text-to-speech software, human dictation, recording. Provide calculators,
graphing calculators, geometric sketchpads. Provide sentence starters or
sentence strips. Use story webs, outlining tools, or concept mapping tools.

Build fluencies with graduated support for practice and performance:
Provide differentiated models. Provide differentiated mentors. Provide
scaffolds that can be gradually released. Provide differentiated feedback.
Provide opportunities to practice with supports and then to show skill
without supports.

Address biases related to modes of expression and communication:
Recognize that learners have preferred modes of expression. Ensure
assessment allows for multiple modes of demonstrating knowledge.

ACTION & EXPRESSION — Strategy Development (Guideline 6)

Set meaningful goals:
Provide prompts and scaffolds for estimating effort, resources, and
difficulty. Provide models or examples of the process and product of
goal-setting. Provide guides and checklists for scaffolding goal-setting.

Anticipate and plan for challenges:
Embed prompts to "show and explain your work." Provide checklists and
project planning templates. Embed coaches or mentors that model think-alouds.

Organize information and resources:
Provide graphic organizers and templates for data collection and organizing
information. Embed prompts for categorizing and systematizing. Provide
checklists and guides for note-taking.

Enhance capacity for monitoring progress:
Provide templates that guide self-monitoring. Provide representations of
progress. Ask learners to identify the type of feedback or advice they seek.
Provide differentiated models of self-assessment strategies.

Challenge exclusionary practices:
Examine practices that may create barriers for certain learners. Ensure
that strategy development supports are accessible to all learners.
