Positioning

What We Are

A personalized curriculum engine that understands how you teach — not just what you teach.

The Sage's Compass helps independent educators create lesson plans rooted in their unique teaching philosophy — whether you homeschool one child, run a micro school, lead a co-op, or teach a learning pod. An assessment reveals your natural teaching archetype, then matches you with curricula and generates lessons that fit your style, your schedule, and your learners. Standards alignment is built in for those who need it, and stays out of the way for those who don't.

One-liner

Interest-driven lesson plans rooted in how you actually teach. Powered by your students' curiosity.

What makes us different

  • Philosophy-specific lesson generation — lessons aren't generic. Each one is generated in accordance with a specific educational philosophy, shaped by your students' interests and your unique teaching style. No other platform does this — competitors mention "works with different philosophies" but generate the same lesson regardless.
  • Distinct teaching philosophies built from quality primary sources on educational philosophy and child development — not marketing buzzwords
  • Teaching archetype assessment — 20 scenarios that reveal your natural teaching style, which connects you to the philosophies that fit how you actually operate
  • Comprehensive curriculum matching against your philosophy profile
  • State standards across all 50 states — available when you need them, invisible when you don't
  • Interactive constellation map to explore the philosophy landscape

We are

  • Philosophy-rooted, grounded in research
  • Empowering educators, not replacing them
  • Personalized to how you teach, not just what you teach

We are not

  • A generic lesson plan generator
  • A replacement for the parent or teacher
  • One-size-fits-all curriculum

Quiz UX Rules

Do

  • Use Cormorant serif for scenario questions — feels like a conversation
  • Progress bar in gold — subtle, warm
  • Choices have generous padding (1rem+) and breathing room
  • Selected state uses gold border + soft gold background
  • Auto-advance after selection with brief 400ms pause
  • Background uses the compass hue (lavender mist / indigo twilight)
  • Keep it one question per screen — no scrolling

Don't

  • Use radio buttons or checkboxes — these are cards, not a form
  • Number the choices (A, B, C) — removes judgment/ranking feel
  • Show philosophy labels on choices (debug only)
  • Use bright/saturated colors for choice cards
  • Make it feel timed or rushed
  • Show a percentage complete — use the thin progress bar only
