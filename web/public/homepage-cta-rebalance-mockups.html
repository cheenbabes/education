Homepage CTA Rebalance

The homepage currently funnels visitors to the Compass quiz, but lessons are what convert. These mockups show targeted changes to surface the lesson-creation path without losing the Compass differentiator.

The diagnosis in one paragraph

The hero headline promises lessons that adapt; the hero CTA asks visitors to take a 5-minute personality quiz before any lesson is shown. That mismatch is repeated across the page: 5 Compass CTAs, only 2 buried Create CTAs. For a parent ready to shop a lesson tool, the quiz reads as a gate, not a gift.

CTA inventory today

DestinationCountProminence
/compass (quiz)5Hero, archetype section, final CTA, footer × 2 — all primary buttons
/create (lessons)2One tiny text link under final CTA, one footer link — never a button
/explore2Mid-page secondary, footer
Sample lessons (just built!)0Not surfaced on homepage at all

What this mockup proposes

  • Surface 1 — Hero CTAs: dual CTAs, with Create a Free Lesson as primary, Compass as a secondary "find your style" option.
  • Surface 2 — Hero visual: swap the abstract archetype ring for a lesson artifact (the actual product) on first paint.
  • Surface 3 — Final CTA: flip emphasis. Lesson creation is the primary action, Compass is the secondary path.
  • Surface 4 — New sample-lessons strip: use the gallery you just built as a zero-signup value demo.
  • Surface 5 — How It Works: reorder so creating a lesson is Step 1 and the Compass becomes an optional Step 0.

All variants are static visual stand-ins. Final copy and exact widths can be tuned during implementation.

Tier 1 changes (highest leverage, lowest effort): Surfaces 1A, 3A, and 4 — together they rebalance the funnel without touching the Compass brand or removing anything substantive. Surfaces 2A and 5A are slightly more involved (visual asset + content reordering) but still self-contained.

Implementation order suggestion: 1) Hero CTAs (Surface 1A) — 30 minutes; 2) Final CTA (Surface 3A) — 15 minutes; 3) Sample lessons strip (Surface 4) — 1–2 hours, mostly reusing /compass/lessons data; 4) Hero visual swap (Surface 2A) — 1 hour; 5) How It Works reorder (Surface 5A) — 30 minutes.

All copy in this mockup is illustrative — final wording is open for revision. The ICP signal worth keeping in mind: Tier 1 is for warm prospects ready to convert; Tier 2/3 (testimonials, pricing teaser, third-party social proof) deserve their own mockup pass.
