Thesis

The whole free tier is a lesson-creation funnel

One loop to rule them all: every path a free user takes should terminate at /create. Browse standards → seed a lesson. Read a sample → seed a lesson. Land on dashboard → seed another lesson. Three lessons per month isn't a cap we apologize for — it's the trigger for paid conversion. When they hit it, they've already felt the quality of our generated lessons and seen two other rooms (progress, scheduling) they'd unlock.

Rules both pages follow:

  • Always unlock: search, browse, read, and turn anything into a lesson.
  • Always gated: anything that attaches to a child, writes state over time (scheduling, completions, progress history), or assumes children exist.
  • Never show bleak emptiness. If a card would read "0 children" or "Nothing yet" on free tier, we replace it with a lesson-creation prompt or an unlock preview. The free user literally cannot add a child — so we don't tease the shape of something they can't fill.
Mockup · /standards · option S-B (finessed)

Standards as a lesson-seed surface

Browsing a state standard should feel like opening a pantry full of ingredients — everything is one click away from a finished lesson. Multi-select lets users stack standards into a single lesson. "Track for child" stays gated because that's where the paid value actually lives.

What's different from before

Pushed further along the "browse → create" axis, and tightened the conversion angle so standards feels inseparable from /create.

Key changes

  • Hero reframes the page as a tool, not a reference: "Turn any standard into a lesson."
  • Philosophy pill up top so users see their quiz result echoed back — "Lessons match your Charlotte Mason style."
  • Multi-select with a sticky bottom tray: pick N standards → one lesson covers them all.
  • Every row's primary CTA goes to /create?standards=…. This already works today.
  • Paid "track per child" collapsed into a single footer teaser — no lock icons screaming throughout.
  • Removed "SEO win" rhetoric — per your call, /standards gets noindex.
Why this drives paid conversion: standards is the page homeschool parents come back to weekly (state compliance is the chore). If we make every visit end in a generated lesson they love, they'll burn through 3 lessons/mo fast — and by then they've seen exactly what tracking would add.

Still gated

  • "Track per child" button on each standard (needs a child).
  • Per-child coverage heatmap (needs children + history).
  • "Auto-mark as covered" on lesson completion.

Mockup · /dashboard · option D-B (finessed)

Dashboard is the user's lesson library, not a progress cockpit

Free users can't add a child, so we don't pretend the cockpit is there-but-locked. The hero is their monthly quota and a big "Create another" button. Below that: their actual lessons (the data they have), their archetype (to reinforce they're in the right place), and one card showing what Homestead adds.

What's different from before

Your feedback: "0 children / 0 completed" looks bleak, and we can't add kids at free tier anyway. Solved by rebuilding around what the free user actually has and can do.

Key changes

  • Hero row replaced with a quota counter + big "Create lesson" CTA. Makes the cap visible every visit.
  • No empty calendar. No "0 children" stat. No dashed preview of per-child progress.
  • Archetype strip reminds the user of their quiz result and links back to sample lessons — reinforces identity.
  • Lessons list shows their lessons with real actions (re-open, print, share). "Schedule" is a chip on each row — honest ask where the value would land.
  • Single "What Homestead adds" card with three concrete benefits. Replaces three locked-blur cards that each nagged separately.
Why this converts: every visit reminds the user how many of their 3 lessons are left. Once the counter hits 0 of 3, the same page pivots (not mocked here) to an upgrade-hero. The dashboard becomes a habit loop: land → see quota → create.

Still gated

  • "Schedule" chip on any lesson row (scheduling is Homestead).
  • Adding children. The page never suggests it's possible on free — only the unlock card offers it.
  • Completion tracking, marking lessons done, progress history.

Resolved

Decisions from the open-questions list

  • SEO indexing of /standards → no. Added <meta name="robots" content="noindex"> guidance. Catalog stays private-by-default; product moat + lesson generation is the pitch, not a scrape-bait standards mirror.
  • Sample-child pattern → skip. Too easy to read as fake. The free tier already has a stronger narrative (your real lessons + your archetype + a cap) and doesn't need a simulated teacher.
  • "0 children / 0 completed" bleak row → removed. Replaced with the monthly-quota hero. The word "children" only appears once on the dashboard — in the "What Homestead adds" card, framed positively.
If these land, next up to build:
  • Wire /standards to be reachable on the free tier (TierGate → public view with gated action chips).
  • Pre-fill /create?standards=… query param support (already supported — verify multi-standard encoding).
  • Wire /dashboard similarly — render quota, lesson library, archetype strip; hide the child picker and calendar for compass tier.
  • Add robots noindex meta to /standards route segment.
  • Instrument events: standards_viewed, standard_seed_clicked, standard_combined_lesson_clicked, dashboard_viewed, dashboard_quota_exhausted_viewed.
